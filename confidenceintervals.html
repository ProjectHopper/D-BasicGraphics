%-----------------------------------------------------% {Confidence Interval}
  • General form of Confidence interval:
    estimate $\pm$ (quantile $\times$ Std.Err)
  • Estimate: sample mean, sample proportion, difference of samples.
  • quantile: quantile from $t$ distribution.
  • Std.Err: Standard error for estimate.
  • Lower and upper bounds are to be calculated.
  • Sample standard deviations and sample sizes are usually relevant.
  • Confidence: $100(1- \alpha)\%$ - use $alpha/2$ and $1-alpha/2$ quantiles. <\ul>

    %-----------------------------------------------------% {Confidence Interval: example}

    • Seven meas urements of the pH of a buffer solution gave the following results:
      5.12 5.20 5.15 5.17 5.16 5.19 5.15.
    • Calculate (i) the 95\% and (ii) the 99\% confidence limits for the true pH utilizing $R$.
    • The standard error is the standard deviation of the sample over the square root of the sample size `n'.
    • $\mbox{Std.Err} = \mbox{std.dev} / \sqrt{n}$ <\ul>

      %-----------------------------------------------------% {Confidence Interval: example} 

      
> x<-c(5.12, 5.20, 5.15, 5.17, 5.16, 5.19, 5.15)
> n=length(x)   #sample size
> alpha = 0.05  #alpha
>
> LB95=mean(x)+qt(alpha/2,n-1)*sd(x)/sqrt(n)
> UB95=mean(x)+ qt(1-alpha/2,n-1)*sd(x)/sqrt(n)
>
> LB95     
[1] 5.137975
> UB95
[1] 5.187739

      %-----------------------------------------------------% {Confidence Interval: example} Thus the 95\% confidence interval for this problem is approximately [5:14; 5:19], while using the same code only taking $\alpha$ =0.01 will give us [5:13; 5:20].
